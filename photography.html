Wedding photography has grown leaps and bounds subsequent to its humble beginnings in the 19th century. The nuanced art transformed soon after the discovery of the first known photograph by Joseph Niepce. During the early times, wedding photography was a luxury for the elite. However, things changed during the mid-century, when it became accessible to the masses.

The 1970s saw a transformation in wedding photography as it became affordable, and the photographers aced at playing around with their individualistic photo documenting style. Classic wedding photography was superseded by photojournalistic photography, which, in due course, unlocked the artistry we witness in wedding photography nowadays. Also, it has been some time since intimate wedding photography surged. Couples nowadays are settling for intimate wedding photography in an intimate setting with narrowed-down guests rather than big-fat weddings.
